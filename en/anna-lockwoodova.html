Story · About the author

Anna Lockwoodová

born Šenšelová · Anna's great-grandniece · creator of this website

Artist, mother of three and discoverer of a family heritage.

Where the past
meets the present.

My curiosity led me to the story of my great-aunt Anna Šenšelová, and so did a find in my grandparents' cellar: two carrier bags full of old embroideries. I had spent years doing embroidery myself (modern embroidery, not traditional folk patterns), so holding these precisely worked pieces of history in my hands filled me with an extraordinary admiration for our ancestors and for the women whose hands could create something like this.

I had never paid much attention to folk culture or traditional dress, but this was the exact moment that changed. I immediately remembered my grandfather's autobiography. I have to admit, with some embarrassment, that I had never actually finished reading it before. Now I understand I simply had to be ready for it. I read his life story in a completely different way this time, devouring it like no bestseller I had ever picked up.

So many of his stories made me laugh. I could picture him perfectly, causing every kind of mischief as a little boy and then as a grown, serious man. What struck me most was that he was not ashamed to name and describe his own mistakes in the book. That raw honesty gave me a kind of freedom, a sense that we do not always have to pretend to be perfect.

Through my grandfather's autobiography, Občas slnko, občas hrmavica (Sometimes Sun, Sometimes Thunder), the memoir of my great-grandfather Ľudovít Šenšel and the publication Šenšelovský rod (The Šenšel Line), I stepped back a hundred years and came to know my ancestors in real detail. But the embroideries stayed in my mind. I wanted to solve the mystery of how they had ended up in my grandparents' cellar and what they actually were.

With more than a little nerves, I contacted Tami Šimončíková Heribanova, who pointed me towards Zuzana Tajek Piešová, a specialist in folk costumes. To my great surprise, she immediately offered to come to my home to look at the embroideries in person.

Zuzka also pointed me towards Stanko Talapka, and when I contacted him I could not believe how willing he was to help. He invited me straight to Martin, where the trail of Aunt Anna led. So I got on the train and went. He told me about the individual embroideries, estimated their origin and use, told me about the Lipa cooperative and even brought along a shirt from their workshop to show me. My father and I also took in a beautiful Karol Plicka exhibition and the permanent folk costume display at the Slovak National Museum.

Just as some things in life seem catastrophic at first, with distance I can see they are sometimes the best that could have happened. In autumn 2025 I was made redundant from a corporate job, and as a mother of three I found it hard to find new work. With each fresh rejection my confidence took another knock. But the period of unemployment gave me the space to explore the family history and the story of Lipa.

In the end I told myself I had to take matters into my own hands. I started teaching myself web design using AI tools. I saw it as the ideal opportunity to turn everything I had gathered during my research into something concrete, and to test new skills at the same time. This way, at least in a small way, I can help make sure the work of our ancestors is not forgotten.

Once I set myself the goal of building this site, my detective work went up another gear. At 38, I got to know my second-cousin-once-removed Ján Jurás, a widely read and deeply enthusiastic man who has been a help from the very first meeting. I also connected with Oľga Kapustová, my third cousin, and I hope this is far from the end.

I believe that knowing your own past is a truly enriching experience. It is like finding the missing piece of a puzzle inside yourself, because every generation before us planted something in us that the next one preserved. And so we are connected by a single thread, even without realising it at first glance.

A little about me

A creative soul

I am a creative soul. After studying marketing at university I spent two years living with my husband in Scotland, in a romantic cottage in the countryside, where our first daughter Amelia was born. When she was three months old we moved back to Slovakia: first to Liptov, then to Bratislava, briefly back to Liptov (where our son Teo was born) and finally, twelve years ago, we settled in Senec. Three years ago our second daughter Charlotte was born there.

Alongside my artistic pursuits I love the garden, baking and our two dogs. When I have any time left I clear my head with a run.

Map of Memories

Since I started getting interested in my own ancestors, people (friends and strangers alike) have been confiding in me about their own remarkable stories of grandparents, great-grandparents and even great-great-grandparents. Every single one is completely unique.

I believe these stories should not be forgotten. That is why I decided to create a map of memories on this website. I would love for you to contribute a piece of your own family past: we can enrich each other with the real lives of the people who shaped who we are today.
