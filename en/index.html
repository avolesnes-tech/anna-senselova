Anna Šenšelová
shop manager, secretary & administrator of Lipa

The story of the cooperative that helped save Slovak folk embroidery

Lipa was not merely a folk craft shop, but a bold women's enterprise — when it was founded in 1910, economic life was almost exclusively dominated by men. Women from the Živena association changed that: they collected old traditional patterns directly from the regions and transferred them onto goods for modern households — children's garments, cushions, tablecloths, embroidered collars, national shirts, leather vests, and flags. Folk embroidery travelled from the villages into the cities, while at the same time providing a dignified income for women who had mastered the traditional arts of embroidery and lace-making. The central figure of this world was my great-aunt Anna Šenšelová — a modest woman with a clear vision of preserving the soul of Slovak ornamentation for future generations.

Map of Memories

It was the discovery of my great-aunt Anna Šenšelová's embroideries that led me to ask: how many such stories are still waiting to be found? That is how the Map of Memories came to be.

An interactive map of Slovakia where each pin represents a real ancestor's story. Share a memory of your grandfather, great-grandmother or distant relative — and help preserve the living memory of Slovakia for future generations. Stories can be searched by surname or region.
